# Week 27 — Revision and the final exam

> **Revision week.** Week 27 of 27 — no new reading, no exercise sheet
> (sheets run 1–26). The final project ends this week.

## What this week is

Everything new has been taught. This week converts twenty-six weeks of
material into exam performance, and it is built around material the course
has been deliberately sitting on:

> **Sealed material.** `y2026p2q3` and `y2026p2q4` have never been opened or
> inspected by anyone. Do not read, extract, or infer their contents.

> **Held back for revision.** `y2023p2q3`, `y2024p2q4`, `y2025p2q4` are
> deliberately unspent until week 27.

The sealed papers are the only questions in the bank that are genuinely
unseen in the strongest sense — nobody, including whoever assembled this
course, knows what is in them. The held-back papers are catalogued but were
never assigned as timed practice or drill, so they are unseen *by you*.
Together they make an honest final possible.

## The exam plan

**Early in the week — standalone timed mock.** Sit `y2026p2q4` under full
exam conditions: 35 minutes, closed book, no prior peeking (it is sealed —
opening it *is* sitting it). Self-mark afterwards against neighbouring
years' papers and your sheet solutions. This is your calibration shot:
it tells you, with none of the familiarity that contaminates re-sat papers,
where you stand.

**End of the week — the FINAL EXAM.** 3 hours, closed book, four questions
of 20 marks in Cambridge Paper-2 style:

| Q | Source | Status |
|---|--------|--------|
| **Q1 — compulsory** | `y2026p2q3` | **Sealed** — opened for the first time inside the sitting |
| Q2 | `y2023p2q3` | Held back — unseen; sat cold |
| Q3 | `y2024p2q4` | Held back — unseen; sat cold |
| Q4 | `y2025p2q4` | Held back — unseen; sat cold |

Attempt all four (80 marks; 140 minutes of question time at the course's
35-minutes-per-20-mark convention — ~1.75 minutes per mark — leaving ~40
minutes for reading, checking and the compulsory question's inevitable
surprises). Mark it the next day, cold, against the
catalogue's per-question mark breakdowns; anything under ~60% on a question
names your last revision target. See
[`../exams/README.md`](../exams/README.md) for the exam-track summary.

This structure spends every reserved question exactly once: one sealed paper
as the mock, one as the unseen compulsory question, and all three held-back
papers as the rest of an unseen final.

## Week load

```
Timed mock y2026p2q4 (sit + mark)      =  1.0 h
Final exam (3 h sitting)               =  3.0 h
Final marking + post-mortem            =  1.0 h
Structured revision                    =  3.0 h
Untimed drill from the pool            =  2.0 h
Project (evaluation + write-up, ends)  =  2.0 h
                                         ------
                                         ~12 h
```

Revision expands to fill whatever it is given; the ~12 h figure is a floor
with the final's adrenaline as the ceiling. The one hard rule: nothing new.
A week-27 hour spent learning fresh material is worth less than the same
hour consolidating what twenty-six sheets already made you produce.

## Notes for the curious

- **`y2020p2q4` is a stretch, not a target.** It rests on I/O buffering theory
  (single/double buffering, circular-buffer sizing) reachable only via week 15's
  Silberschatz §12.4.2/§12.4.4 cross-reading. If you did that reading properly it
  is a fine extra timed rehearsal; if not, it is not worth learning new material
  for in exam week.
- **`y2008p1q2` is abandoned on purpose.** It is pure data representation (two's
  complement, floating point) — an artefact of the era when the OS questions
  shared Paper 1 with computer architecture. It is not OS material and is not part
  of this course.
- **Why the reserved papers were reserved.** A revision week needs unseen
  material, and unseen-ness is a non-renewable resource: every question
  assigned as weekly practice is spent forever. The course therefore spends
  its weekly timed papers mostly on older stock (plus spaced re-use of
  memory papers in weeks 23–26) and keeps the three most recent **unspent**
  fully-covered questions, plus both sealed 2026 questions, for this week.
  A few recent papers are deliberately spent en route — weeks 8, 13 and 22
  sit `y2024p2q3(a–b)`, `y2023p2q4` and `y2025p2q3` — which is why the
  reserve is the most recent *unspent* trio rather than simply the newest.
- **After the exam:** OSTEP's closing *Summary* dialogue was read in week 24
  (the book's other dialogues land earlier — ch. 52's in week 19, App. A's in
  week 25); the book's real last word is Appendix G/H's project list, and the
  course's is your project write-up. If you want somewhere to go next,
  [`further-reading.md`](./further-reading.md) lists four papers that
  re-read the course rather than extend it — optional, and deliberately not
  part of this week's hours. If the course worked, the exam is the least
  interesting thing you produced this year.
